Scallion Oil Noodles (Cong You Ban Mian)

Servings: 2
Total Time: 15 mins

Ingredients

  • Â¼ cup neutral-tasting oil, avocado, canola, peanut, etc.
  • 6 scallion
  • 2 tablespoon light soy sauce
  • 3 tablespoon dark mushroom soy sauce
  • 1-2 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 100 gram noodles of choice, 2 servings
  • Sesame seeds for garnish

Steps

  1. Wash and pat dry the scallions, do not skip the drying step, this is to prevent the oil from splattering later.
  2. Trim off the roots, chop the white and semi-green stem parts into 3 inch long pieces, save the darkest green leafy part.
  3. Slice the scallion white and semi-green parts again lengthwise into thin strips, and finely chop the dark leaf part.
  4. In a wok or frying pan, heat the oil over medium-low heat, add the scallion whites first, stir and fry for about 2 minutes until slightly golden, then add the semi-greens, lower the heat and let them fry slowly.
  5. Once almost all the stalks are golden brown, start straining out the crispy browned pieces and set aside.
  6. In the scallion oil pan, add the light and dark soy sauce and sugar, keep the heat low and stir constantly to incorporate the oil and the sauce.
  7. Meanwhile, bring a pot a water to boil and cook your noodles according to your preference, I like to keep them a bit on the chewier side, the longer you cook them the softer they get.
  8. When ready to serve, pour a couple tablespoons of scallion oil sauce over the noodles to your taste, add back in the crispy fried scallion pieces, stir to combine well, garnish with chopped scallion greens and sesame seeds, enjoy!
